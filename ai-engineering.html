ENGINEERING PHILOSOPHY

PHILOSOPHY

Everything I build started as a manual process first. These principles are just patterns I kept running into while doing the work.

🧠
Real-World Context
Systems that understand nuance. The same content can be harmful in one context and educational in another. My architectures embed that judgment from day one.
⚡
Velocity at Scale
Moderation at 100k+ cases/week demands efficiency. Every data structure, every API endpoint is optimized for the throughput and latency constraints of real platforms.
🛡️
Safety by Default
When a system controls user safety, defaults matter. I know humans will miss edge cases, I missed plenty, so redundancy, escalation paths, and full audit trails are baked in from day one.
📊
Observable & Explainable
Automated decisions need explainability. Systems include telemetry, audit trails, and enough transparency to justify enforcement actions to users and regulators.
🤖
AI-Assisted Development
I'm not a traditional coder and I don't pretend to be. I use Claude to plan and architect, DeepSeek and Qwen via Aider for the actual code, and I pick the cheapest model that can do the job. What I ship is real and live. That's what matters.

AUTOMATION WORK

BUILDING WITH AI

I built the Outreach tool because job hunting is repetitive and most of the steps can be automated. Here's how it actually works.

🔍
Job Discovery Pipeline
Playwright opens job boards and career pages on a schedule, BeautifulSoup parses the listings, and anything relevant that I haven't seen before gets added to a Google Sheet. No manual searching.
🤖
Resume Tailoring via Claude
For every new listing, I send the job description and my base resume to Claude API. It rewrites the summary and adjusts the highlights to match. By the time I look at it, it's already tailored.
✍️
Auto-Fill Automation
Playwright navigates to the application page and fills in the fields. Name, contact info, experience, the tailored resume copy. Works on most standard job portals.
📊
Outreach Dashboard
All of it surfaces in a custom frontend at outreach.bynipun.com. Same stack as ModEval, plain HTML/CSS/JS.
SYSTEM DESIGN

SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE

This is how ModEval is structured under the hood. Six layers, each one solving a specific problem I ran into while building it.

⚙️ Core Layers
01
Data Ingestion & Normalization
Content comes in from everywhere - Reddit, Twitter, LLM outputs, user reports, each in its own format. The first layer standardizes all of it, tokenizes it, creates one clean internal format. You can't make good decisions on garbage data.
02
Multi-Model Evaluation
A single model is never enough. ModEval queries 5 independent safety classifiers simultaneously, capturing disagreement patterns. Consensus indicates high-confidence violations; disagreement signals edge cases requiring human review.
03
Context Enrichment
Raw model scores lack context. The system retrieves historical account data, community norms, user reports, and violator intent signals. A word that's slur in one community might be reclaimed language in another.
04
Decision Scoring & Thresholding
Models give you probabilities. Thresholds turn those into yes/no decisions. But the cost of getting it wrong varies wildly - a false positive on hate speech hits different than one on spam. Configurable thresholds let each platform find their own comfort zone.
05
Action Enforcement & Escalation
Clear-cut violations get removed automatically. The close calls go to humans. Some cases need specialists - CSAM always gets routed to the child safety team, no exceptions.
06
Audit Trail & Explainability
Every decision gets logged with full reasoning - which models flagged it, what context we had, what threshold we used. You need that trail when someone appeals. Explainability isn't nice-to-have, it's mandatory.

DeepSeek · Qwen
