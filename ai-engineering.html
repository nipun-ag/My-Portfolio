SYSTEM ARCHITECTURE

How automated safety and sentiment systems are designed for reliability, explainability, and scale. Each layer reflects a specific domain requirement.

⚙️ Core Layers
01
Data Ingestion & Normalization
Content comes in from everywhere - Reddit, Twitter, LLM outputs, user reports, each in its own format. The first layer standardizes all of it, tokenizes it, creates one clean internal format. You can't make good decisions on garbage data.
02
Multi-Model Evaluation
A single model is never enough. ModEval queries 5 independent safety classifiers simultaneously, capturing disagreement patterns. Consensus indicates high-confidence violations; disagreement signals edge cases requiring human review.
03
Context Enrichment
Raw model scores lack context. The system retrieves historical account data, community norms, user reports, and violator intent signals. A word that's slur in one community might be reclaimed language in another.
04
Decision Scoring & Thresholding
Models give you probabilities. Thresholds turn those into yes/no decisions. But the cost of getting it wrong varies wildly - a false positive on hate speech hits different than one on spam. Configurable thresholds let each platform find their own comfort zone.
05
Action Enforcement & Escalation
Clear-cut violations get removed automatically. The close calls go to humans. Some cases need specialists - CSAM always gets routed to the child safety team, no exceptions.
06
Audit Trail & Explainability
Every decision gets logged with full reasoning - which models flagged it, what context we had, what threshold we used. You need that trail when someone appeals. Explainability isn't nice-to-have, it's mandatory.
